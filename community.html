Reaching Every Atoll

Since 2009, Dr. Aishath Ali Naaz has travelled to every corner of the Maldives — delivering free workshops, training, and public lectures on mental health, parenting, violence prevention, and social wellbeing. Her community work has reached over 62,000 people across all 19 atolls and over 98% of inhabited islands.

Timeline

A History of Service

2024–2026
Weekly TikTok uploads on social and psychological issues affecting children, parents, and youth — bringing mental health education to a new generation through digital media.
2020–2021
Delivered 52 free Facebook Live sessions during the COVID-19 pandemic, covering parenting, compassion, wellbeing, lockdown coping strategies, quarantine mental health, and adapting to the new normal.
2019
Appeared on 22 episodes of #haasdhuniye on Raajje TV, creating public awareness on mental health issues affecting Maldivian society.
2018
Appeared on 25 episodes of #haasdhuniye on Raajje TV and 16 episodes on national radio programme #nafsanee dhulhaheyokan (mental wellbeing). Conducted free workshops across five islands of Kaafu Atoll on preventing gang, domestic, sexual, and drug-related violence.
2016
Produced and presented Dr. Naazge Nazarun — 16 episodes on Raajje TV covering gang violence, domestic violence, bullying, sexual abuse, and mental health. Delivered free workshops at K. Atoll School.
2015
Led a free mental health camp in Aa. Ukulhas, providing psychological profiling for 100 people with a team of 10 professionals.
2012–2014
Rehabilitated 69 gang members through free counselling, group therapy, and family interventions. Designed and delivered the Bridge to Peace and Bridge to Faith workshops.
2009–2026
Ongoing travel to all 19 atolls delivering free workshops, training, and lectures to students, youth, religious scholars, parents, and community leaders.
2004–2005
Designed and delivered three Certificate Courses in Basic Counselling Skills for de-addiction counsellors (680 hours each).
2001–2005
Conducted youth counselling courses, life skills and parenting workshops across 11 atolls, and developed the first family counselling manual.
