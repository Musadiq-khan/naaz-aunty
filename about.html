About

The
Architect
of Maldivian
Mental Health

Dr. Aishath Ali Naaz is the Maldives' foremost consultant clinical psychologist a scientist, advocate, and pioneer who has shaped her country's mental health infrastructure for over a quarter century.

Her doctoral research at the University of Manchester explored the relationship between psychosis, anger, aggression and violence in South Asian subcultures drawing data from India, Sri Lanka, and the Maldives. That work formed the foundation of nationally-informed clinical and policy interventions still in use today.

As founder of MIPSTAR — the Maldives' first private mental health centre — she built a two-floor clinical and training institute serving clients island-wide. She was the first psychologist to give expert evidence in Maldivian courts and has authored over 17,000 psychological profiles across her career.

Background

A Life Devoted to Healing

Early Life & Formation

Born and raised in the Republic of Maldives, Dr. Naaz pursued her undergraduate studies at Bishop Cotton Women's Christian College in Bangalore, earning a First Class degree in Psychology, Sociology and English Literature. Her academic excellence continued with a PG Diploma in Journalism where she ranked first nationally, before pursuing clinical specialisation at NIMHANS one of South Asia's foremost psychiatric institutions.

Doctoral Research

Her PhD at the University of Manchester investigated the complex relationships between psychosis, substance abuse, anger, aggression, and violence within South Asian subcultural contexts drawing on primary data collected across India, Sri Lanka, and the Maldives. This landmark research positioned her as one of the most authoritative voices on mental health in the region.

Academic & Literary Life

Beyond clinical work, Dr. Naaz is an accomplished novelist and writer twice winning the Maldivian National Award for Fiction Writing. She has produced and presented television and radio programming on mental health, reaching hundreds of thousands of viewers across the archipelago through her landmark TV series Dr. Naazge Nazarun.

Building National Infrastructure

In 2000, Dr. Naaz founded ADAPTS — the first dedicated mental health service in the Maldives, bringing together a psychiatrist, clinical psychologist, and specialist educators under one roof. Over two decades, she expanded this into MIPSTAR: a two-floor clinical and training institute serving clients across all 17 inhabited atolls.

Policy & International Work

Dr. Naaz has served as National Consultant to the Ministry of Health and WHO Maldives, developing the country's Mental Health Strategic Action Plan 2016–2021. She has presented at over 30 international conferences across six continents, collaborated with UNICEF, UNDP, and the Strong Cities Network, and sat on human rights bodies of the Maldivian government.

Community Service

Her community engagement has reached over 52,000 individuals through free workshops, public lectures, and outreach campaigns. During COVID-19, she delivered 52 free Facebook livestreams on mental health and wellbeing. She personally rehabilitated 69 gang members through free counselling, group therapy, and the 'Bridge to Peace' programme — creating tangible pathways to education and employment.
