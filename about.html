About

The First
Clinical
Psychologist
of the Maldives

Dr. Aishath Ali Naaz is the first Clinical Psychologist of the Republic of Maldives — a practitioner, researcher, advocate, and pioneer who has shaped her country's mental health infrastructure for over a quarter century.

Her doctoral research at the University of Manchester explored the complex relationships between psychosis, substance abuse, anger, aggression and violence in South Asian subcultures, drawing primary data from India, Sri Lanka, and the Maldives. That work formed the foundation of nationally-informed clinical and policy interventions still in use today.

In 2000 she founded ADAPTS — the Maldives' first mental health service, bringing together a psychiatrist, clinical psychologist, and specialist educators. In 2001, ADAPTS opened the country's first dedicated mental health clinic. On 10 April 2011, she established MIPSTAR — the first private mental health practice in the Maldives — now a two-floor clinical and training institute employing nine staff and serving clients across all 19 atolls.

She was the first clinical psychologist to provide expert evidence in Maldivian courts, and has authored over 17,500 psychological profiles — including over 300 victim-of-crime profiles — reaching more than 62,000 people across over 98% of the country's inhabited islands.

Background

A Life Devoted to Healing

Early Life & Formation

Born and raised in the Republic of Maldives, Dr. Naaz pursued her undergraduate studies at Bishop Cotton Women's Christian College in Bangalore, earning a First Class degree in Psychology, Sociology and English Literature. Her academic excellence continued with a PG Diploma in Journalism where she ranked first nationally, before pursuing clinical specialisation at NIMHANS — one of South Asia's foremost psychiatric institutions.

Doctoral Research

Her PhD at the University of Manchester investigated the complex relationships between psychosis, substance abuse, anger, aggression, and violence within South Asian subcultural contexts — drawing on primary data collected across India, Sri Lanka, and the Maldives. This landmark research positioned her as one of the most authoritative voices on mental health in the region.

Building National Infrastructure

In 2000, Dr. Naaz founded ADAPTS — the first dedicated mental health service in the Maldives, bringing together a psychiatrist, clinical psychologist, and specialist educators under one roof. The first mental health clinic opened in 2001 under the ADAPTS banner. On 10 April 2011, she established MIPSTAR — the first private mental health practice in the Maldives — now a two-floor clinical and training institute serving clients across all 19 atolls.

Academic & Literary Life

Beyond clinical work, Dr. Naaz is an accomplished novelist and writer, twice winning the Maldivian National Award for Fiction Writing. She has produced and presented television and radio programming on mental health, reaching hundreds of thousands of viewers across the archipelago through her landmark TV series Dr. Naazge Nazarun.

Policy & International Work

Dr. Naaz has served as National Consultant to the Ministry of Health and WHO Maldives, developing the country's Mental Health Strategic Action Plan 2016–2021. She has presented at over 30 international conferences across six continents, collaborated with UNICEF, UNDP, and the Strong Cities Network, and sat on human rights bodies of the Maldivian government.

Community Service

Her community engagement has reached over 62,000 individuals through free workshops, public lectures, and outreach campaigns across all 19 atolls and over 98% of inhabited islands. During COVID-19, she delivered 52 free Facebook livestreams on mental health and wellbeing. She personally rehabilitated 69 gang members through free counselling, group therapy, and the 'Bridge to Peace' programme — creating tangible pathways to education and employment.
